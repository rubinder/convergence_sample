The problem

Two delivery methods, sold as one.

Internal WBD ad-sales teams have run linear and digital as two separate systems for years. Convergence collapses that: manipulate broadcast and streaming as one inventory, without making anyone reconcile two tools — and without double-counting a viewer who was reached in both.

The metric

Reach, at three grains.

Reach is the count of unique individuals exposed within a window. Frequency inflates raw counts; the whole job is to dedup people correctly — across days and across audiences.

Exact

Daily reach

count(distinct individual_id) read straight from a Gold snapshot — fast enough for a dashboard tile.

Approximate · merged

Cumulative reach

HyperLogLog sketches merged across days → deduped uniques, with no rescan of raw impressions.

Approximate · merged

Cross-segment reach

Sketches merged across audiences → unique individuals deduped between segments — the incremental-reach question.

Why HyperLogLog

Sketches beside the counts.

Each Gold row stores an HLL sketch alongside the exact count. That one design choice is what lets reach compose: any window, any set of segments, merged at query time in Athena/Presto — never re-scanning billions of raw impressions. A daily compaction job keeps the Iceberg tables tight across bronze, silver, and gold.

store  cast(approx_set(individual_id) as varbinary)
merge  cardinality(merge(cast(hll_sketch as HyperLogLog)))
